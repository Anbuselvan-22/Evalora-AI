Question 1: Explain the process of photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy. 
This process occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells and involves several key steps:

1. Light Absorption: Chlorophyll in the leaves absorbs sunlight
2. Water Splitting: Water molecules are broken down to release oxygen
3. Carbon Dioxide Fixation: CO2 is converted into glucose
4. Glucose Production: Sugar molecules are created for plant energy

The overall chemical equation for photosynthesis is:
6CO2 + 6H2O + Light Energy → C6H12O6 + 6O2

This process is essential for life on Earth as it produces oxygen and forms the base 
of most food chains. Photosynthesis occurs in two main stages: light-dependent 
reactions and the Calvin cycle.

Key factors affecting photosynthesis include:
- Light intensity
- Carbon dioxide concentration  
- Temperature
- Water availability

Without photosynthesis, most life on Earth would not exist as it provides the
oxygen we breathe and the energy that flows through ecosystems.
